University of Maryland students balance work and school with part-time jobs


By Elana Renbaum

Having a job while being a college student is a path most students go down at the University of Maryland, College Park.

In a recently conducted poll of 30 students, about 90% of pollers said that they have a job while pursuing school full-time. For junior Jenna Dawson, having a job while being a student allows her to financially support herself and fund her education.

Dawson works with the International Rescue Committee to help settle new refugee families in the area.

“I help families adapt to their placements here in the states [in areas like] English comprehension, financial literacy, etcetera,” she said. In addition, she also is an intern for the government and teleworks from her Leonardtown apartment.

Like Dawson, around 30% of respondents hold two jobs in addition to school. About half of the respondents have one job during the school year.

Senior Shane Connuck holds three jobs during the school year: a lacrosse beat writer for the Washington Post, a play-by-play commentator for Big Ten Plus, and a writer and public relations contact for Maryland Athletics. He enjoys these jobs because of the real-world experience he is getting outside of the classroom, while still being a college student.

Some students work where they live- juniors Faith Antonetti and Natalie Davis work for Resident Life, Antonetti as a Resident Assistant and Davis as a multicultural advocate.

For Antonetti, the decision to be an RA was a no-brainer. She gets to create a welcoming environment for new residents and gets paid to do so.

“I’m an RA so it pays for room and board as well as dining for me. It is important to me because it makes college much more affordable and I also enjoy building the community,” she said.

Davis works to, “have professional working experience as well as to make some income to supplement college fees.”

Not everyone is in it for the money. Sophomore business student Samantha Ferguson works as a marketing intern for Maryland Athletics and isn’t paid for her work. She is currently the secondary marketing contact for the softball team and helps to plan fan entertainment.

“It allows me to gain experience in my field of interest. I also enjoy it,” she said. “My favorite part is being able to make people happy through the experience.”

"I have been involved with sports for long that I understand the importance of them and if I can make someone’s experience better, I strive to do that,” - Sophomore Samantha Ferguson

“The entire goal is to make an atmosphere that is unforgettable,” Ferguson said.
